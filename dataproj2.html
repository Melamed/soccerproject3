Top 20 selection mistakes made by top European soccer clubs since 2011 (according to data)

by Dmitry Melamed

Even though top soccer clubs usually have world-class scouting systems, they do sometimes make mistakes. If you ask regular soccer fans which transfers in European soccer were the biggest failures in their opinion, they would probably give you some names from this list. But I went further and decided to settle this question once and for all and give an answer using the most accurate method - data analysis.

For almost each player that got transferred to top European clubs since the 2011/2012 season I found out what was the transfer cost, managed to figure out what their approximate salary by September 2014 was and calculated their average rating for their respective clubs up until this point. Players who no longer play for the clubs, played less than 5 games, or got transferred just this winter are not on the list because it is either impossible to find all relevant data for them or because the statistics that are out there would not be a fair assessment of their actual level.
By calculating their mean deviation from the average rating(6,9) and salary(816 thousand pounds) among them I was able to determine a deviation factor that ultimately helped me create this top 20. Enjoy! And Premier League fans get ready, the majority of players in this list are from English clubs.

Built on data taken from: transfermarkt.co.uk; whoscored.com; Footbal Manager 2015 and various other sources on player salaries.

1. Radamel Falcao

Club Monaco / Manchester United
Transfer from Atletico Madrid in May 2013
Transfer cost 52,8 million pounds
Salary circa 2014 ~ 3,15 million pounds a year
Average rating for Monaco / Manchester United 6,77
Deviation from mean rating -0,13
Deviation from mean wage + 2,334 million pounds

Isn't this a surprise? I guess not. As data shows, Radamel Falcao's transfer to Monaco and loan spell to Man Utd are the top two biggest selection failures made by top clubs in the last four years. A good excuse for Man Utd is that they didn't buy him yet. A bad excuse for them is that they are paying him a salary of 275 thousand pounds a month, which totals to a ridiculous 3.3 million pounds a year. Not to shabby for a reserve team player, huh? His performance at Monaco is however, a surprise indeed. When creating this data set, I expected the Colombian's season at the Ligue 1 club to be in the list, but not this high. All in all, we should all feel sorry for this guy (even if you are a United hater). He used to be a fantastic player two years ago but something is not going well since that fateful knee injury. But, for sure, modern soccer would benefit if Falcao will once again return to his true level. Let's hope that happens soon.
